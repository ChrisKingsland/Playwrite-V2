Step 1: Dictionaries *

Built-in dictionaries
Personal dictionaries
Add a dictionary

You can upload your own dictionaries for Magic Fill to fill your grids with words that you prefer.

The format of a dictionary file is simple: one word per line, with each word optionally followed by a semicolon and a score. Words with a higher score are more likely to appear in suggested grids.

These dictionaries will be stored only in this browser. They will not be accessible on any other browser, even if you are signed in to the same PuzzleMe account.

Note: Dictionaries for Indian languages have to be installed by us and cannot be uploaded directly. Please email puzzlemaster@amuselabs.com if you have such a dictionary.

Drag and drop the files here or click to upload
Formats supported: TXT, DICT
Maximum file size - 20 MB
Blocklists
Add a blocklist

You can upload your own block lists for Magic Fill to remove all the words in it from the final list, even if it is in any of the selected wordlists.

The format of a block list file is simple: one word per line.

These lists will be stored only in this browser. They will not be accessible on any other browser, even if you are signed in to the same PuzzleMe account.

Drag and drop the files here or click to upload
Formats supported: TXT, DICT
Maximum file size - 20 MB
Set score cutoffs
You can use these cutoffs to control the quality of the Magic Fill grids. Magic Fill will exclude all words below the minimum word score. Similarly, it will exclude grids with words whose scores total up to less than the minimum grid score.

Enter the number of floating black boxes, a maximum of 10. Magic Fill will place these black boxes for you. Tips for Beginners

The larger the grid, the more black boxes you need to get a good fill. Using floating black boxes can help Magic Fill because it can choose where to put the black boxes to generate high-scoring fills. However, it can also take more time to run and may slow down your computer. For a 5x5 grid, we recommend a range of 0 to 2 floating black boxes. If you are trying to fill a large grid with many black boxes, you should fix the places of some black boxes first, and leave only a few floating black boxes for Magic Fill.

Step 3: Must-have words

Enter one or two must-have words, separated by commas. Magic Fill will ensure these words are placed in the grid. It will also prioritize related words, highlighting them in orange.


Need Rebus, Indic or Schrodinger words?

To fit multiple letters in a single cell, enclose those letters in curly brackets. This is useful for rebus cells (Example: PUZZLE{ME}) or to pack letters into a single grapheme in Indic scripts (Example: अ{म्यु}स{लै}{ब्स}). To provide a Schrodinger word for magicfill separate the two variant words with a slash (Example: CAT/DOG).
